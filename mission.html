Our History

In the halls of Tufts University, two environmentally conscious students, Emily Harper and Alex Mitchell, shared a common passion for sustainability and a deep concern for the planet's future. Bonding over their shared values, they envisioned a world where fashion could be both stylish and sustainable.

In 2015, Alex and Emily founded EcoEssence from their dorm rooms. With a mission to redefine fashion through eco-friendly practices, they set out to create a brand that would prioritize sustainability at every step of the production process.

Utilizing their academic backgrounds in environmental science and business, Alex and Emily worked tirelessly to research and implement sustainable materials and ethical manufacturing practices. They sourced organic cotton, recycled polyester, and other eco-friendly fabrics to create their first line of clothing, all while ensuring fair wages and safe working conditions for their suppliers.

Their innovative approach to fashion caught the attention of eco-conscious consumers, and before long, EcoEssence began to gain traction in the market. As the years passed, EcoEssence continued to grow, expanding its product range and forging partnerships with like-minded organizations. From launching a zero-waste initiative to planting trees for every purchase made, Alex and Emily remained dedicated to pushing the boundaries of sustainability in the fashion industry.

Today, EcoEssence is dedicated to redefining fashion by seamlessly blending style with sustainability. Our mission is to create clothing that not only looks good but also does good for the planet. We strive to inspire conscious consumerism and foster a community committed to preserving our environment for future generations.

Our Goals

Implementing a zero-waste manufacturing process by 2025, utilizing innovative recycling techniques and sustainable materials. Achieving Fair Trade certification for all supply chain partners within the next three years to ensure ethical treatment of workers and support local communities. Reducing water usage by 50% in production processes by investing in water-saving technologies and promoting water-conscious practices throughout the company.
